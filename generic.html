Yes, Interactive maps
are worth the trouble

Did not mean to shout at you but yes, interactive maps are worth the time.

Static maps can only hold and potray so much information. If I had opted for a static maps, I would be limited to sharing a part of the picture.

Interactive maps support the user in understanding information from multiple levels. In the case of my crime map, you get to assess regions that are prone to violent acts against women and children from a general country country level to the more smaller level of neighbouring cities and towns. I do acknolwedge the short coming of the data. Since the crimes were recorded in relation to the main city of the county I can only hope to partner with data centres in the future to record such data in a more detailed structure that could allows records be related to the initial police post that recorded an offence.
