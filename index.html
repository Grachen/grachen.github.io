Interactive maps in support of local research efforts

Project Proposal

Making interactive maps for a beginner in R studio can feel a bit overwhelming in the intial stages.
And this only gets worse after commiting to learn because the frustrations just pile up when you start comparing and longing for the convenience associated with mapping in platforms like QGIS or ArcMap. So what to do?
Well,it is a good thing we can always return to static maps.

That answer is right up there with the most chosen route of action but I will share my very simple mantra when wanting to learn mapping in R.Someone else has done it, so go find that code and move on from there.

Selling Cartography

I like simple, informative and clean maps, so i can describe myself as a bit of a conservative cartographer . But when I made this map , I went a tad outside of my comfort zone and used a black backdrop and I loved it. Now I enjoy the freedom of bold, complementary and contrast colours but not too much yet. I have a feeling it is because of my mentor (whom I respect tremendously for helping me mould myself cartographically) but never wanted to see purple or any "crazy colours" when we made maps.

Another proud element for me was the title. I decided on a local dialect because I would love to see more local terms on information products, especially for rural schools: Be the change you want to see, so feel free to reach out and partner with me.
